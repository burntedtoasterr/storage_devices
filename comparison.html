  COMPARISONS


   

   -Price
   One gigabyte of SSD Storage can be as expensive as two gigabytes of HDD Storage
   -Storage Capacities
   Most HDDs have at least 256GBS of storage and can go up to more then 4TBs,
   unlike SSDs which begin at 128GBs
   -Life Span
   Most HDDs last longer then SSDs.
   -Speeds
   SSDs are a lot faster then HDDs at reading and writing files
   -Durability
   SSDs are more durable as HDDs are more prone to mechanical damages due to
   their multiple moving parts
   -Power Consumption
   SSDs use less power then your typical HDD
   -Noisyness and Temperature
   HDDs make more noise and heat then SSDs due to their spinning platters
   -Form Factors
   HDDs are bulkier then SSDs making them unusable in smartphones and other small
   electronics

FUN FACT: Consumers bought around 739.7 million gigabytes of hard-drive storage space last year.  


   Conclusion

   In our opinions, we find that SSDs would be the optimal storage device in most situations.

   It's sleek form factor allows it to be used in more devices and the no noise and/or vibrations really seals the deal. The faster boot times
   that SSDs provide for computers are incredibly useful and the quicker loading of data also helps in some applications, such as video
   games. We think it's optimal usage would be as the boot drive, with mainly the applications that need to load and/or read data fast,
   whereas a harddrive could be used as a secondary drive for other files that don't require as much speed.

   So In Conclusion, even though SSDs have less storage, if you have the money it is probably better to go for an SSD(in most situations).
