  HARD DRIVES (HDDS)


   What Is A Hard Drive?

   A hard drive is a computer component used to store files. This type of storage device
   Is used in many places including desktop PCs, laptops, smartphones, etc. Hard-drives
   use disk-like platters that spin at high speeds(usually around 7000 revolutions per
   minute(rpm), however some higher performance models can go up to 10,000 rpm)
   to store data. To access data there are read and write heads which are controlled
   by motors that can magnetically write or read information from said platters.

FUN FACT: Facebook was storing close to 300 PB of data on hard drives in mid-2014. 


   History of the Hard Drive

   Hard drives were first created in 1953 by engineers at IBM. These engineers
   were trying to create a way to be able to have random access to high capacities
   of data at a low cost.

   The first drives developed had around three megabytes of storage and and was around
   the size of a refrigerator. As time went on, hard drives evolved to be denser
   while also increasing their storage capacities. By the mid-1980s,
   they were small enough to finally become a standard in personal computers(PCs).
   Today, hard drives can hold terabytes of data, and are usually only
   a few centimeters thick.

FUN FACT: A 256 GB smartphone is around 54 Olympic-sized swimming pools full of 1958-era HDDs.
